Research Projects

My research explores generative modeling under severe information constraints, with a focus on perceptual fidelity, representation learning, and human-centric evaluation.

Perceptual Video Compression with Diffusion Models

Problem

Video traffic dominates global internet bandwidth, creating critical challenges for real-time transmission in bandwidth-limited environments. Ultra-low bitrate (ULB) video delivery in the range of 0.005â€“0.02 bits-per-pixel remains poorly addressed by both classical codecs and existing learned video compression methods. Traditional codecs such as H.264 and HEVC, optimized for pixel-level distortion, suffer from severe perceptual degradation at these rates.

Method

I developed the first diffusion-based learned video codec capable of operating effectively in the ultra-low bitrate regime. The key idea is to eliminate conventional inter-frame residual encoding and instead transmit only sparse, high-level semantic priors at the encoder. These include latent keyframes and lightweight motion trajectories.

At the decoder, a controllable diffusion model reconstructs temporally coherent intermediate frames, shifting complexity from transmission to generation. This enables realistic video synthesis even at extreme compression rates.

Architecture

Encoder: Encodes sparse semantic priors and motion trajectories.

Decoder: Uses a controllable diffusion process to synthesize inter-frames.

Key Contributions

  • A semantic-guided generative video codec that enables perceptually faithful reconstruction below 0.02 bpp by synthesizing inter-frames at the decoder.
  • A trajectory-aware synthesis module that leverages sparse motion priors to improve temporal coherence, yielding smoother perceptual degradation and lower LPIPS/FID.
  • A comprehensive evaluation against H.264, HEVC/VVC, and state-of-the-art learned codecs across both distortion and perceptual metrics.

Evaluation

Experiments on standard benchmarks demonstrate that the proposed approach significantly outperforms classical codecs in perceptual quality at ultra-low bitrates, highlighting the potential of generative compression for sustainable and human-centric video communication.
